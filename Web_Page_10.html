Hyperlinks

Knowing how to link your code to other documents is important.

Absolute References

Absolute references(sometimes called external links)connec to pages in other domains. In the href attribute of the tag the full UrL of the page, image, or other resource you would like to link to is specified. One example might be your quest to list some of the greatest bakeries of all time. SInce you want to link to the bakeries themselves and not files you wrote, you would use an absolute refernce.

  • Washtenaw Dairy
  • Rosie's Coffee Bar and Bakery
  • Dexter Bakery

An advantage to absolute references is that you are always linked to the most up-to-date information on that page. A disadvantage is that it is up toyou to make sure that the links don't change.

Relative References

Relative references (sometimes called internal links_ connect to pages in other domains. In the href attribute of the tag only the folder(if any) and filename is specified. Some examples of when you might use relative references would be in your navigation bar or links to your images.

  • Donuts from the Squire shoppe in Ashtabula Ohio

Images as Links

Many and more people are moving away from textual prompts to ones that are graphics based. This is because the page looks nicer. I can redo the link above using a picture instead of worrds.

Something to keep in mind though, is that everyone can see the image, or they may not realize that the image is a link. As you become more confident in your coding you will want to make sure to have many people test your pages so that you can make it an optimal experience for as many people as possible.

Embedded Links

Every tg can have an id attribute. The id attribute gives a name to a specific section of the DOM. You can use that id to link directly to a part of a page instead of just a page in general. This is a great way to allow people quickly jump from one part of your page to the specific section you are looking for. Review the material on:

  • Absolute References
  • Relative References
  • Images as Links
  • Embedded References

When you click on any of the links above, the browser will bring that section into focus. It may be the case that the bottom two links don't do anything for you. That is because this content is already in your screen.
